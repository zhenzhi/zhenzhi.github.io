User Identity

Context

What is identity?


Identity refers to the set of information we have that identifies a user as a unique individual. This includes, but is not limited to, identifying information such as their name and address, their authentication credentials, and the authorization they have to engage with our services.

Why identity is important?


Good identity benefits users, it can help them to manage their account like the sign in or change information easily. Good identity also benefits business, it can streamline the sales with easier add-ons. It can also improve customer care, with less need to gather the info. A good identity system can have better security to combat fraud.

The project


Since I joined AT&T, my major responsibility is redesigning the new registration experience to make sure users can create their account easily and safely. Internally, we called this project Sakura. Sakura is a cheery blossom lives short but beautiful, which represents what we want for registration. We believe registration should be a quick and beautiful experience for users.

My Role


I am the only UX designer in the team who handles all the design work including wireframe, prototype, and all the other handoffs. I worked very closely with one lead designer who manages the direction of the project, one UX researcher who in charge of all the research works and also one content strategist who responsible for the copy in the UI.

What we know at the beginning

50% of customers are unsuccessful in creating an AT&T user ID and password.
$ 21.4M per year in tech time assisting customers with broadband registration.
$ 192K per year on chat agents to assist broadband registration.
In one word, the current registration experience is horrible. There's a lot of space we can improve.

Design and research Round 1

What we know


I spent a lot of time dig in the quantity data we have and worked very closely with the researcher to understand what’s the issue with current experience.
There’s a lot of users who drop when they need to enter the account number and passcode. From the user interview, we understand that the reason they don’t enter that information because they don’t know where they can find the account number and they forgot when they have created passcode and what is it.

What we propose


Since users got trouble with account number and passcode, why don’t we get rid of them?
We proposed to use first and last name, date of birth and zip code to register.

what're the Results

For this design, we got a strong pushback from the backend team. First of all, we can not guarantee this data set is unique for all the users. Secondly, the backend is not designed to find an account like this way, it will take a huge amount of time to change it. Thirdly, all those data users need to enter here are easy to get to someone who knows them, which will cause security issues.

Design and research Round 2

What we know


We learned the lessons that we definitely to understand the backend system better. So I planned with the researcher to speak with different stakeholders to understand the system better. We found out that different AT&T services use different backend systems to manage users' information. For example for wireless service, it usually uses phone numbers with account numbers or passcode to identify users, but for the internet, it usually uses account numbers and passcode to identify users.

What we propose


Since different services have different ways to identify users, why don’t we customize the registration flow for different services? We proposed to direct different users to different registrations. And we gave users some guidance on how to find their account number and passcode.

what're the Results

We present this design to stakeholders, they agreed on the design and happy about that. But as a designer, I start to doubt if this the best we can do and is this solve users’ problem. Wireless users don't have to enter a passcode, but for other users, they still need to enter the account number and passcode. Although we have some design to help them to find that information, it won’t help a lot of users. I do think in this design we compromised too much to technology constraints and it may not be the best solution to users.

Design and research Round 3

What we know


I planned and worked with the researcher, content strategist and other designers in the team to have brainstorm sessions and workshops to ideate the possible solutions for registration. We came up with some really good ideas, an email-based identity system is the one that stands out.

What we propose


Since we can use email to identify users’ account information and verify them at the same time, why don’t we use that? What users need to do is just click the link in the email and create a password then they finished the whole registration.

what're the Results

Most of the users are happy to see this design. Some users are concerned about spam email. Most of the stakeholders on board with this design. Some stakeholders are concerned about the timeline and budget to do that.

To be continued


Now we figured out what’s the right direction for the registration process. We need to think about how do we make this happen because registration is not a standalone piece, it highly depends on other parts like sales, customer service, and security. For example, one of the proposals we have for the sales team is to communicate better the reason we need users’ email addresses is for them to create an account, not for email marketing. We are still working with different teams to make sure this design can be built successfully.

FUTURE IMPROVEMENT AND THOUGHTS

A good designer is a good communicator. Other people probably only see the final interface, but there are so many things designers need to work on to make that happen. Most of my time is spent on understand and communicate the projects. I used to think designers can focus on their craft, but after working on this project, I noticed that knowing how to communicate to let yourself and others to understand is as much important as your design crafts.

Good design is good business. When we consider deferent designs, we are not only thinking about what the best for users, but also what’s best for the business. If we want to get buy-in from stakeholders, we always need to understand the business and show the value proposition in the design.
