How people can sign up for internet
—on the internet


NEW INTERNET PURCHASE EXPERIENCE

CONTEXT

Introduction

This is a 10 weeks concept discovery project I have done at AT&T. Our team was trying to find the opportunities to make it easy to sign up AT&T internet online. We believe an internet sign up experience should be:
Easy. It’s the most frictionless way for customers to get internet.
Comprehensive. It reflects a holistic customer experience, from landing page to order confirmation.
Complete. We get all the data the business needs, while customers accomplish everything necessary for a great experience going forward.

Project Goals


• Decrease the fall-out rates in each purchasing step
• Reduce the cost of customer support
• Build customer trust and increase customer satisfaction rate

Roles and responsibilities

I was the interaction designer in the team who handled the problem-solving design work like using wireframe and prototype to demonstrate and explore different ideas. I worked very closely with one design lead who managed the direction of the project, one visual designer who was in charge of visual direction including design components and illustrations, one UX researcher who was responsible for all the research works and also one content strategist who edited and reviewed the copy in the UI.

Design and research Landing page

What we know

What we propose - Iteration 1

From research, we knew users have a hard time to understand offer details. So in our new design, we separated offer details into three sections, contracts, devices, and installation. HBO Max was about to release at that time, so we thought showing HBO Max promotion could be a way to provide more values to AT&T internet.
We tested this version with users, users liked the clear and fresh look and feel, they also thought it’s easy to scan all the information there. But they also mentioned since they were trying to buy the internet, they wanted to know about the internet itself than promotion.

What we propose - Iteration 2

In this second version, we removed the promotion part and focused on the internet itself. We want to make sure customers understand AT&T internet is high quality and they are clear about what they will get.

Design and research Order summary

What we know

What we propose - Iteration 1

In design iteration 1, we were trying to itemize the payment including service fees, devices fees, promotions, and taxes. We separated billing into what’s due today and what’s due monthly.
From user testing results, users liked the itemized section, and they felt clear on what they were paying, but they were not sure when they have to do the monthly payment.

What we propose - Iteration 2

In iteration 2, we added the frequency and date when users need to pay the bill. We also simplified the item and switched the order of due today and due monthly to make it more clear to users.

Design and research Creating account

What we know

What we propose - Iteration 1

In the previous experience, users would receive an email to create an account after purchase. From research, we knew this is not a good experience because users would ignore the email and they have to create it at the time they have to sign in account to pay the bill. So in this new design, we let users create their accounts during the purchase, so when the purchase is done, their account is ready for them.
From the user testing, users mentioned they preferred and liked to get an account created during the purchase. But they also mentioned that passcode and security questions are hard to remember.

What we propose - Iteration 2

Passcode and security questions are out of date identity verification methods. In our second iteration, we replaced that with email and phone number verification. So users won’t need to remember their passcode and security question. They can just get the code sent to their email/phone to verify themself. For password creation, we reduced the rules to create it and also used 2-Factors Authentication to secure the account.

Design and research Credit info

What we know

What we propose - Iteration 1

In the previous design, users need to enter SSN to get a credit check and the system will hard pull their credit which will affect their credit score. And if their credit score is not high enough, they have to pay a deposit ahead. In this first iteration, we let all the users pay the deposit ahead and they don’t have to enter SSN to do the credit check.
From user testing, we knew that users are confused about how deposit going to work even though we explained in the text. We also got pushback from the risk department that they mentioned AT&T needs credit info to reduce the risk of churn.

What we propose - Iteration 2

In this new design, we removed the concept of deposit, instead, we let all the users pay the first-month bill at the time they made the purchase. We had to add the credit check for the risk need but this time we made the credit check soft pull, which won’t affect users' credit score.
