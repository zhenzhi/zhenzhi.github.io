Field Operation
Technicians Message

Context

The Product - Atlas






Atlas is an app that supports AT&T skilled field technicians on the job. It’s like their digital tool belt. With Atlas, technicians can view their assigned install and repair work, fill in the necessary information, and testing the internet before they close the job.

The requirement

As a manager, I need the ability to message techs within Atlas in order to avoid circle back communications and ensure important messages are received and read.

My Role

As the only researcher and designer in the project, work closely with product manager and engineer lead, in charge of end to end design.

Design and research Round 1

What I know

Based on the interview and discussion I have with stakeholders, it seems like a helpful feature for technicians and managers. With this feature, technicians can receive direct updates from their managers to make sure they are not missing something important. So I start with quick wireframes and prototypes and plan to test with users.

What I propose

what's the Result

After testing with technicians, I got some important findings and good insights from them. Technicians feel it’s totally not necessary to have this message feature since they already can get messages from managers by SMS message and email. They have a huge concern that this will become a way their managers can micro-manage them and distract them from the work they are working on.

Design and research Round 2

What I know

From the previous testing, technicians brought a good point that whether this feature is necessary or not. I planned another round research session to understand this requirement better. I conducted 9 interviews with managers to understand what kind of message they want to broadcast to the technicians and why they feel Atlas is the best platform to do that. I also created a survey and send it to more than 50 technicians to get a broad opinion on this feature.

What I propose

Based on the research I have done, it direct to the conclusion that this is a feature that is not necessary and potentially will make the relationship between managers and technicians worse. So I came up with a message strategy and tried to convince the stakeholders.

what's the Result

Stakeholders especially those managers still think it’s necessary to show some of the key info like covid-19 updates or tech procedure update. They suggest that we can educate managers to make sure the message they send is not sounded micro-managed.

Design and research Round 3

What I know

I don’t believe educating managers will be effective and that change will happen in a short time. I am more believe in using design and technology to constrain bad behavior. So I need to find a way to show the important message and don’t make it to interrupt the technicians’ workflow.

What I propose

what's the Result

4 out of 6 Technicians like this approach that doesn’t impact their workflow and get notified of critical info. Stakeholders agree with this solution which minimizes the interruption and reduces the concern technicians have.

FUTURE IMPROVEMENT AND THOUGHTS

Real data helps. I used “lorem ipsum” at the beginning. This fake content lets me miss the point that what kind of messages managers trying to send. It always better to use real content and data.

Don’t miss the product vision. A lot of times we feel adding more features to a product can make it better. But sometimes it will make us lose our focus. The message feature shouldn't prioritize highly than the major workflow. Need always keep in mind what’s the vision of the product and who is that for.
