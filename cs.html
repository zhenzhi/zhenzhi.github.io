Summary

Coursmart is a platform designed for online course users to find the best fit courses. Itâ€™s also an online community where users can exchange their learning experiences with each other within the topic they are interested in.

Backgrounds

MOOCs, the acronym for Massive Open Online Courses, have been heralded as the next big revolution in higher education. They are transforming the educational landscape, with already millions of people enrolled across hundreds of courses from top institutions through various provider platforms (e.g., university consortiums Coursera and edX). Regardless of where you live, your background and level of expertise, if you are interested in expanding your knowledge with online courses, MOOCs are the best places to go. With hundreds of new courses coming online every year and little or no feedback about their quality, the issue of how to find the courses which are right for an online learner taking into account his/her schedule, interests, and long-term career aims becomes pressing. For instance, from the description, the course might sound to be a practical course but after going through 2 or 3 modules, you find out it is a theoretical one. In order to save several hours for online learners to discover whether the courses are designed for them, we decided to design and prototype a crowdsourced online course review platform.

Informative: Reviews

Reviews speed up your decision making process. You can filter search result by reviews as well as find helpful reviews by matching your background to that of reviewers, determining whether the course meets your needs.

Various: Share Wall

A picture is worth a thousand words. Uploading a picture of your project or achievement you got from the course could help other online students have a better overview of this course.

Personalized: User Profile

Creating your user profile. Getting to know each other. You can edit your academic background and interests, and grant access permission of your LinkedIn profile to other members on Coursmart. In addition, you could employ Coursmart to manage all your courses by synchronizing the information from your online course platform account.

Supportive: Online Community

For each course, Coursmart connects online students and creates a vibrant learning community: students can see all the learning progress of their classmates, post questions, and answer othersâ€™ questions.

Process

We have used IDEO's design thinking methodology for this project.

Contextual Interviews

In the contextual interview phase, 5 people were interviewed, each interview took about 30 minutes with 7 semi-structured interview questions. The interview questions were designed to gather information of the intervieweeâ€™s experience with online courses and their decision making process and outcomes. For example, what kinds of courses they would like to take and how they found the courses. Moreover, we also asked them about their experience with writing and sharing courses ratings and reviews.

User Survey

We launched a survey created in Qualtrics and posted it on the Insider Listserv from Oct. 14th to Oct. 23rd. The survey is comprised of 17 questions. We were motivated to find out online learnersâ€™ experiences of choosing and taking courses, including why they would consider taking a course, why they drop a course, and whether/how they share a course with others. There were 76 respondents completed the survey and most of them ranged in the age from 18 to 34. 97% of the respondents are students or professionals.

Affinity Diagramming

After we gathered the data from the contextual interview, survey and In-depth interview, we wrote our data points in the post-it notes and organized them to find the insights from the data we have gathered.

User Journey Map

We generated a user journey map from our data collection. With this user journey map, we will be able to interpret the overall story from an individualâ€™s perspective of their decision-making process and the actions they would take when taking an online course. We pointed out four different aspects - stage, goal, activities, and touch points (including pain points and opportunities).

Target Audience

Based on the analysis of the interview and survey data, we defined our target audience. We are trying to solve the problem users have, not our owns.

Storyboard

In our storyboard, we wrote up a story about the interaction between Jordan and Coursmart, including the problems he met, the way he found out Coursmart, and how Coursmart solved his problems and helped him to succeed. This storyboard helped us to identify the challenges we want to tackle and crank out the solution in the next step.

Iterative Design

We created 4 versions of prototype and conducted 4 rounds of usability testing. The tools we mainly used are Balsamiq, Marvel App, Sketch, InVision, and Adobe Photoshop. In each round, we thought the problems we want to solve, made the prototype to ideate design and checked by user testing to validate design.

Usability testing

We conducted 4 round usability testing to validate Coursmart's design. The low-fidelity and medium-fidelity prototype are conducted by person by person. The high-fidelity prototype are conducted remotely through UserTesting.com. We measures Coursmart's performance by SUS(System Usability Scale). The industry average score is 68, and we got a score much better than that, 82.5. It's an A.
