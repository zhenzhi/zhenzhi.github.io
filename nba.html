What is Saved Payment Checkout

PayPal Saved Payment Checkout(Billing Agreement) is one of the most important PayPal checkout solutions integrated by lots of big merchants like Wayfair, Uber, and Starbucks. With the PayPal saved payment checkout in place, it is no longer necessary for you to log in and enter payment information for each purchase.

Project Goals

Increase the PayPal selection rate among other payment methods
Improve customer satisfaction on saved payment checkout

My Role

I am the only designer on this project. I lead all the design work and collaborate and facilitate all the conversations with product managers, content designers, researchers, engineers and partner teams.

The Before

First-time set-up experience has a 68% conversion rate due to many clicks and challenges with comprehension.

 

 

 
Second-time (subsequent) experience has a 95% conversion. We still can improve the experience by giving users more visibility on which card to use and switching payment methods.

 

 

 

 

Redundancy
Users have to set up payment again and again on different merchants, and that redundant process isn’t valuable.

 

 

Transparency
On the second time experience, users can’t see which card will be used to pay. Plus, we have automatic wallet retry on payment, but users may not be aware of how it works or know they signed up for it.

 

 

Flexibility
Choosing a preferred card on the first-time setup, not on return (second-time) experiences doesn’t allow full wallet access to pay. Users are not able to choose other payment methods when they need to, and this isn’t flexible long-term.

 

HMW




How might we remove the friction of setting up a PayPal payment at every saved payment merchant & enable a seamless checkout experience across our network?
