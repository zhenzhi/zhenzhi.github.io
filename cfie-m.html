CHINESE FOOD IS EASY

Summary

CFIE is a Chinese food recipe mobile app. This app can teach people interested in cooking Chinese food how to cook it easily and quickly. This app optimized the process of preparing and making food. It also have many sweet features like read recipes and timer to help users to use recipes easily. In a world, CFIE - The end of kitchen disaster.

Problem Space

The number of Chinese students enrolling in American universities has grown to nearly a quarter million and preparing meals that remind them of home can be difficult and time-consuming for a number of reasons, such as limited availability of Chinese ingredients, the complexity of recipes, limited transportation options, and searching inefficiencies on existing recipe apps. Students are busy, they don't want to spend a lot of time making foods. The Chinese food is cooked with the intention of increasing the health benefits like longevity and having healing powers and medicinal value. More and more people like Chinese food but they think Chinese food is hard to make. Actually, making Chinese food could be easy. We want to make an application easier to use for people who interested in cooking Chinese food.

what should I eat today?

Daily recommendations based on time. Various recipe choices. Breakfast, lunch, dinner, dessert. Popular, local and video recipes.

how can I find the recipe I want easily?

User centred search function. Browse, sort and filter the result, find the best match.

how can I use this recipe easily?

Step by step directions with text and image. Read recipes, convert unit, timing. Recommend similar recipes.

how can I prepare for my food?

Add ingredients to your shopping list. Never forget something.

Process

Contextual Inquiry and Interviews

2 people were observed when they were cooking by the direction of Chinese food recipe. In the contextual inquiry part, we are more focusing on how people using recipe when they are cooking. 4 people including Chinese and American students were interviewed. We have gathered data about how they cook and what kind of problems they met during cooking.

Competitive Analysis

We have used competitive analysis method to compare four different recipe apps including two Chinese versions and two American versions. In this part, we are more focusing on how to navigate the content, how to search a recipe and how to present a recipe.

Usability testing

We conducted 2 rounds usability testing to validate our design like search process and content. Specifically, we were interested in how test participants navigated the homepage and search results pages, their expectations regarding filter options, and how users expected to engage with the site. Our test participants ranged in age from 22-38, including graduate students from China, India, France and United States. Based on the usability issues we found and feedbacks from users, we changed our design iteratively.
